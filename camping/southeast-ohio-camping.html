Best Camping in Southeast Ohio

Camp near Hocking Hills waterfalls, caves, and gorges, plus scenic lakes in Ohio's hill country

Explore Southeast Ohio's Premier Camping Destinations

Southeast Ohio is home to the state's most dramatic landscapes, centered around the spectacular Hocking Hills region. Camp near stunning waterfalls, deep gorges, and ancient caves carved into sandstone cliffs. This area offers everything from state park campgrounds with modern amenities to private sites just steps from iconic trails. Whether you're exploring Old Man's Cave, hiking to Cedar Falls, or enjoying the peaceful lakes dotting the rolling hills, Southeast Ohio provides unforgettable camping adventures in Ohio's most scenic terrain.

Essential Camping Tips for Southeast Ohio

Peak Season Planning

Hocking Hills campgrounds fill up months in advance for fall foliage season (September-October) and summer weekends. Book as early as possible through Ohio State Parks. Consider weekday visits for better availability and fewer crowds on trails.

Trail Proximity

Hocking Hills State Park campground is closest to the main trails, but private campgrounds like Old Man's Cave Chalets offer walking distance access. Salt Fork and Burr Oak provide excellent hiking with fewer crowds than Hocking Hills.

Weather & Terrain

Hill country means cooler temperatures and more rain than northern Ohio. Pack layers and quality rain gear. Trails can be slippery after rain. Spring brings waterfalls at peak flow, while fall offers stunning foliage.

Hiking Preparation

Trails feature steep gorges, stone steps, and uneven terrain. Wear proper hiking boots with good traction. Bring plenty of water and snacks. Popular trails like Old Man's Cave can be crowded on weekends - arrive early or visit weekdays.

Food & Supplies

Logan is the main town near Hocking Hills with grocery stores and restaurants. Stock up on supplies before reaching your campground. Many remote areas have limited cell service, so plan ahead and download maps offline.

Photography Tips

Best photography is early morning or overcast days when waterfalls and caves are evenly lit. Tripod recommended for low-light cave interiors. Fall foliage peaks mid-October. Respect trail closures and stay on designated paths.
