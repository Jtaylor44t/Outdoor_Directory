Best Parks in Central Ohio

Explore Columbus Metro Parks, nature preserves, and scenic gorges in Central Ohio's diverse park system

Discover Central Ohio's Outstanding Metro Parks and Natural Areas

Central Ohio is home to the excellent Columbus Metro Parks system, dramatic river gorges, and diverse nature preserves. From the sandstone cliffs of Blackhand Gorge to the bison herds at Battelle Darby Creek, this region offers incredible outdoor experiences with easy access from the Columbus metropolitan area. Explore prairies, wetlands, effigy mounds, and extensive trail networks.

Essential Park Visiting Tips for Central Ohio

Metro Parks Access

All Columbus Metro Parks are completely free with no admission fees or parking charges. Parks are open from 6:30 AM to dusk year-round. No permits or reservations needed for general park use and trail access.

Trail Networks

Metro Parks offer over 230 miles of trails total. Download trail maps from the Metro Parks app or website before visiting. Most parks have multiple trail options ranging from easy to moderate difficulty levels.

Bison Viewing

At Battelle Darby Creek, view bison from designated observation areas only. Never approach the fence or try to feed wildlife. Best viewing is typically early morning or late afternoon when bison are most active.

Urban Accessibility

Many parks are accessible via COTA bus routes. Scioto Audubon is walkable from downtown. Consider biking to parks on the extensive Central Ohio trail network for car-free adventures.

Nature Programs

Metro Parks offer free naturalist programs, guided hikes, and educational events. Check the online calendar for upcoming activities. Nature centers at several parks feature exhibits and live animals.

Year-Round Activities

Winter offers sledding at Blendon Woods and cross-country skiing. Spring features wildflowers and bird migration. Summer is perfect for water activities. Fall showcases spectacular foliage throughout the parks.
