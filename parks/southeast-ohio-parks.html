Best Parks in Southeast Ohio

Experience dramatic Hocking Hills landscapes, large state parks, and forested hill country in Southeast Ohio

Discover Southeast Ohio's Dramatic Landscapes and Remote Wilderness

Southeast Ohio is home to the spectacular Hocking Hills region with its waterfalls, caves, and dramatic rock formations, along with some of Ohio's largest and most remote state parks. This rugged, forested hill country offers exceptional hiking, backcountry camping, and opportunities to experience true wilderness. From the iconic Old Man's Cave to the vast expanse of Salt Fork, Southeast Ohio provides unforgettable outdoor adventures.

Essential Park Visiting Tips for Southeast Ohio

Hocking Hills Crowds

Hocking Hills is extremely popular, especially on weekends. Visit on weekdays or early mornings for smaller crowds. Fall foliage season (October) and summer weekends are the busiest times. Arrive early to secure parking at popular areas.

Trail Conditions

Southeast Ohio trails can be steep, rocky, and challenging. Wear proper hiking boots with good traction. Trails may be slippery after rain, especially near waterfalls. Bring trekking poles for steep descents and creek crossings.

Seasonal Highlights

Winter features frozen waterfalls and ice formations. Spring brings peak water flow and wildflowers. Summer offers lush greenery and swimming opportunities. Fall showcases spectacular foliage in the forested hills.

Lodging Options

Reserve lodge rooms and cabins months in advance for popular dates. Several parks offer modern lodges with restaurants. Backcountry camping available at Burr Oak and other locations. Hocking Hills has numerous private cabins nearby.

Remote Areas

Cell phone service is limited or non-existent in remote parks. Download maps before arrival. Bring paper maps and compass. Let someone know your plans. Some parks like Tar Hollow are truly remote wilderness areas.

Wayne National Forest

Many Southeast Ohio parks connect to Wayne National Forest lands, offering extended hiking opportunities. The Buckeye Trail passes through the region. Explore state forests adjacent to parks for additional backcountry experiences.
