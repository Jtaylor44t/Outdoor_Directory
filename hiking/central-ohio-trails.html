Best Hiking Trails in Central Ohio

Discover Columbus Metro Parks, nature preserves, and scenic trails along rivers and gorges in Central Ohio

Discover Central Ohio's Metro Parks and Nature Preserves

Central Ohio offers an exceptional network of hiking trails centered around the Columbus Metro Parks system. From the dramatic sandstone cliffs of Blackhand Gorge to the peaceful wetlands at Glacier Ridge, the region provides easy access to diverse natural environments. Experience scenic river valleys, preserved gorges, and abundant wildlife just minutes from Ohio's capital city.

Central Ohio Hiking Tips

Metro Parks System

Columbus Metro Parks offers 19 parks with over 250 miles of trails. Most are free, well-maintained, and within 30 minutes of downtown Columbus. Get a Metro Parks passport and explore them all!

Urban Wildlife

Despite proximity to the city, Central Ohio parks host abundant wildlife including white-tailed deer, bison, bald eagles, and great blue herons. Early morning hikes offer best viewing opportunities.

Seasonal Flooding

Spring rains can cause flooding along creek and river trails, especially at Blackhand Gorge and Battelle Darby. Check trail conditions before visiting March through May.
